About
Wenchang Yang

I am currently a postdoc in Prof. Gudrun Magnusdottir's group in the department of Earth System Science at UC Irvine, with research interests covering East African hydroclimate variability and change, large-scale climate variability and dynamics, ITCZ, extratropical storm tracks, baroclinic wave and wave packets, monsoon circulation, etc.

Before coming to UC Irvine, I received my B.S and M.S. from Peking University in China, supervised by Prof. Benkui Tan; and Ph.D. in atmospheric sciences from Columbia University, with the dissertation titled The Hydroclimate of East Africa: Seasonal cycle, Decadal Variability, and Human induced Climate Change, supervised by Prof. Richard Seager and Prof. Mark A. Cane. My Ph.D. work also involved a lot of collaboration with Dr. Bradfield Lyon at IRI of Columbia University.

A range of computer tools are being used in my research: Python and Matlab for computation and data analysis, LaTex for typesetting, Beamer for presentation.
